UX Research Project

UX + System Design

MATLAB App for Behavioural Experiments

Designing a touch-based experimental platform to run controlled studies, integrating audio and video stimuli, survey questions, instructions, countdown timing, and response recording.

Overview

Role

Lead Researcher & Designer

Users

Participants (normal hearing) · Researchers

Duration

2 months

Platform

Computer with touch screen

Methods

Observational usability testing

Task-based evaluation

Participant interviews

Iterative refinement

The Challenge

Design an app that supports uninterrupted long-duration experiments while maintaining participant comfort and data quality.

Interface was visually distracting and difficult to use

Constant changes during the study broke flow and increased error

Only one condition could run at a time

Long sessions created uncertainty and fatigue

What I Did

What the app needed to do

The full experimental flow was structured to support continuous sessions, clear interaction, and reliable data capture.

Designed end-to-end experiment flow

One-hour study structured into sessions, conditions, breaks, and outputs.

Refined touch interaction design

Larger touch targets and calmer visuals improved clarity and ease of interaction.

Integrated experiment components

Speech tasks, surveys, breaks, and instructions combined in one system.

Automated data logging

Responses, reaction time, and accuracy recorded in structured format.

Controlled visual conditions

Consistent light levels maintained to protect physiological measurement.

Key Insights

Interface clarity reduces load

Interaction must not compete with task difficulty.

Behaviour shows more than words

Hesitation revealed unclear interaction and instruction.

Interruption affects data

Small disturbances can change experience and physiological response.

Long sessions need pacing

Breaks and transitions were part of the design, not extras.

Design Decisions

Design choices shaped by observation and interviews

Experiment Flow

Manual switching during study

Constant changes during the study broke flow and increased error.

Automated progression across sessions

Continuous and standardised execution.

Break Timing

Unclear waiting time during breaks

Participants did not know when the next part would begin.

Countdown with user-controlled continuation

Clear pacing and reduced uncertainty.

Video During Break

Content could influence later response and physiology

Uncontrolled content risked affecting later measures.

Neutral video with no narration

Reduced emotional influence during breaks.

Screen / Background Design

Screen with high contrast

Risked eliciting stronger emotion and affecting measurement, especially pupil diameter.

Calm background with controlled screen light

A calmer visual design was introduced, with screen light kept consistent and mild.

Interface Clarity

Small targets and distracting layout

More effort required to find and select responses.

Larger elements and simplified interface

Reduced search time and cognitive load.

Impact

Faster studies. Higher retention. More reliable data.

Outcome

Reduced study time

Study duration reduced by around 50%, with sixty one-hour experiments completed in two months (typically four to five months).

High retention, low dropout

Dropout rate reduced from 17% (expected) to 3% (only one dropout out of thirty participants).

Uninterrupted sessions

One-hour experiments completed without intervention.

Reduced workload and error

No manual adjustments required for complex components.

More reliable data

Less emotional and physical disturbance during measurement.

Reflection

Reflection

Real-session observation revealed issues not reported afterwards. Immediate note organisation and hard-copy backup remained important in a digital workflow.
